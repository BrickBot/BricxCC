Macros

Besides templates, BricxCC also offers macros to quickly add pieces of text to your code. A macro consist of holding down the <Ctrl> and the <Alt> key (and sometimes the <Shift> key) and then pressing another key. For example, if you press <Ctrl><Alt>A the text OUT_A is added to your code. Macros behave similar to templates and many templates are also available as macros. By default the following macros are defined:

<Ctrl><Alt>AOUT_A
<Ctrl><Alt>BOUT_B
<Ctrl><Alt>COUT_C
<Ctrl><Alt>1SENSOR_1
<Ctrl><Alt>2SENSOR_2
<Ctrl><Alt>3SENSOR_3
<Ctrl><Alt>Iif construction
<Ctrl><Alt>Hwhile construction
<Ctrl><Alt>Eelse construction
<Ctrl><Alt>FOnFwd();
<Ctrl><Alt>ROnRev();
<Ctrl><Alt>OOff();
<Ctrl><Alt>LFloat();
<Ctrl><Alt>WWait();
<Ctrl><Alt><Shift>Ttask definition
<Ctrl><Alt>><Shift>Ssub definition
<Ctrl><Alt>><Shift>Vvoid (= inline) definition
<Ctrl><Alt>><Shift>Iint definition

You can see a complete list of macros defined in the Macros tab in the Preferences window. Here you can also change the macros and add new macros.
